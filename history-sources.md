## Primary Interviews (video transcripts)

### Brixton Documentary Interview (Norman & Ralston Grant)
Filmed in Brixton, London. Both brothers interviewed.
- Early life in Falmouth compound: 10 shillings/month, 2 families sharing 1 kitchen; "poor but happy"
- Sang in Anglican Sunday school choir from age 5–6
- Made instruments from sardine tins, milk cans, fishing line, frying pans, chamber pot (chimmy)
- Grandfather played fiddle; father sang folk songs and church songs on weekends
- Name "Twinkle Brothers" given by a Rasta in Uggingstown yard
- 1962: Won parish competition; 1963 reached regional final in Montego Bay — claim to have been robbed; 1964 won
- 1968 & 1969: Won Gold medals at National final
- Ralston went to Cobbler Camp (like a prison), met Family Man (Aston Barrett) in the choir there
- Norman went to Kingston for auditions: Coxson, Prince Buster, Beverly's, Treasure Isle
- Stayed with relatives in August Town and Trenchtown; slept on floors; "just eat dumpling and butter"
- First solo record 1964; first group record 1966 with Duke Reid — "I Don't Like You!"
- Met Bob Marley, Desmond Dekker, Jimmy Cliff, Alton Ellis, Bunny Lee in Kingston
- The Cardinals: first band, Falmouth, hotel circuit, 6 nights/week while at school
- 1970 Festival Song Contest: placed third; told to come to Bunny Lee next day
- Randy's Studio: mastered same day, pressed next day, paid in records
- First Soul Syndicate session was with the Twinkle Brothers
- Bunny Lee taught them PRS, publishing, business side
- 1974: Sessions with Sly Dunbar, Lloyd Parks, Ancell Collins
- Rented Treasure Isle from Duke Reid — first time he ever rented it out
- "Jahovia" and "I Love You So" with Family Man and Carlton Barrett
- R: "1974 became Rastaman — saw the sail in the moon"
- N: "Was about self-reliance"
- 1975: Mexico City with Sonny Bradshaw — first international exposure
- Carib Gems UK: ~1972–73; 5000 copies considered good; *Rasta Pon Top* (1975) on Carib Gems
- 1976: Four songs; DJ Sir Lee took to Virgin; played to Simon Draper and Richard Branson
- *Love* — first 10" reggae record in England; 5 sessions in the 70s for Virgin
- Virgin stopped reggae; started Twinkle label 1981; took UK residency 1986
- 1980: Toured with Inner Circle; 1981 first UK tour; 1982 USA (Roxy LA, Roger Steffens)
- 1982: Signed for Sunsplash JA — haven't performed in Jamaica since
- 1986: Wlodek Kliszcz from Poland; studio £1.20/hour vs £24 in England; 8 albums made
- Bought 17 leather jackets at £5; sold for £75 in London
- Trebunie-Tutki: lived with them a week, Anna (16) translated; lay drum and bass first then violin
- 4 albums from collaboration; 1991 Poland tour; *Girl Guide* film (1995)
- 2006: went back, picked up Gold record; 1995 set up London studio with Poland money

### Norman Grant interview by Maken, 2023 (Poland)
URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-61thKYEt_o
- Norman born 1950; Ralston born ~1948
- "That creativeness bloomed in all of us" — on Jamaica's independence in 1962
- Norman 12, Ralston 14 when they entered the 1962 festival
- 1968: Won All Island Festival — two gold medals (Twinkle Brothers + Norman Grant solo)
- Toots and the Maytals entered the festival professional side "four or five times"
- From age 14 (1964): singing American soul, calypso, English pop in hotels
- Influences: Impressions, Stylistics, Temptations, Smokey Robinson, James Brown, Funk, Jazz, Calypso
- "That was a good training ground — knowing all those melodies helps you create your own style"
- The Cardinals: lawyers, accountants; Norman & Ralston "the only ghetto youth in the band"
- Schubert and the Miracles → Celestial band in Montego Bay (~8 years, £20/week)
- Made 2 tourist hotel albums; one recently sold for £150 on vinyl from America
- Did "Little Green Apples" in reggae before Dennis Brown's version
- Rastafari: "From around 14 or so, you have Rasta men all around"
- 1966: Haile Selassie came to Jamaica — Norman already making music by then
- "As the Majesty did say — you would be like a ship without a rudder"
- Virgin: three deals in England total; Virgin the third and best
- Punk compilation: Sex Pistols, Twinkle Brothers, Gladiators on one album
- First went to England 1975; music already popular there
- No headphones in Duke Reid's studio 1966; Norman had to turn away from the piano
- "I think the music was better those days"
- Norman writes all Twinkle Brothers lyrics himself: "The word is the power"
- Poland childhood memory: cheapest shoes in Jamaica made in Poland
- Paid in millions of złoty; bought leather coats at 5 złoty → sold in London for £85
- Met Kliszcz at Jah Shaka's shop in London
- Underground LP with Solidarity logo — came out 1984
- Spirit Studio Zakopane: ganja growing outside
- "I told Judah: we have to create our OWN beat. Don't follow them."
- Bank account in Poland; money funded London recording studio in 1995
- Plans to bring Trebunie-Tutki to Jamaica; plans to open a youth club
- "Every day I make music — even doing this interview"
- Last performed in Jamaica 1982: "It's getting to the time now"

### L'Astrolabe Orléans interview (ASTROTV, date unknown)
URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dhSU8qZ8idg
English auto-generated captions
- Poland since 1988; Kliszcz came to London 1986, worked on Polish radio
- "Twinkle Brothers were popular in Poland — reggae music was very popular in Poland"
- Studio: "only £1.20 an hour for a 24-track studio — in England at least £24. One pound 20 was a good deal."
- Made about 8 albums in Poland
- Trebunie-Tutki: "people from the mountains, Zakopane — Polish cultural people, roots"
- Went to Poland in '91 with Dub Judah; *Higher Heights* album; then *Songs of Glory* "three or four years ago"
- "In Jamaica was '82. We haven't performed in Jamaica since 1982."
- On independence: "To be independent in music you don't have a company to tell you what to sing. Independently, you're singing from your heart."

### Ferme de Gwernandour interview, 18/11/2017
URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=igw-vnJi2wQ
- Ralston's Rasta conversion (1974): "I got up one morning early and it was a full moon — in the shadow, Selassie appeared right in the moon. I was kind of scared to go back in the house. He turned like into a little baby, about ten years old. I said, well, stick on the locks."
- Prince Buster's studio: "The Maytals were recording — after they finished, time's up. We never got to record."
- Duke Reid: "He liked us and recorded us. Said 'you have a nice little Christian song.'"
- "We were going to the studio singing 'I'm hungry, fire for Babylon' — sad songs but we're happy, dancing off for sadness. For some reason the world loved it."
- "These songs are not going to tell you to take a gun and kill that man because he is wicked. We can tell you that you're wicked — with a smile."
- 1970 Festival Song: came third; while with Bunny Lee recorded about 14 songs

## Published Sources

### Wikipedia — Twinkle Brothers
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Twinkle_Brothers
- Formed 1962, Falmouth, Jamaica
- First single "Somebody Please Help Me" (1966), Leslie Kong
- Late 60s–early 70s hotel circuit; debut album *Rasta Pon Top* (1975)
- 1977: Virgin/Frontline deal — *Love*, *Praise Jah*, *Countrymen*
- Early 80s: Virgin contract ended; Norman moved to UK; established Twinkle label
- Collaboration with Trebunie-Tutki since early 90s (referenced in academic texts)
- Discography to *Pass It On* (2019)

### Wikipedia — Jah Shaka
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jah_Shaka
- Born Neville Powell, Clarendon Parish Jamaica, c.1948; died 12 April 2023
- South East London-based sound system from 1970
- "Zulu Warrior" — the definitive UK roots/dub sound system
- Followers included Public Image Ltd, The Slits; influenced drum & bass, dubstep
- Ran the Culture Shop community hub in New Cross (record store, Caribbean food, Rastafari hair salon)
- **Close personal friend ("bredren") of Norman Grant**
- **Collaborated on *Revelation 18* in the early 1980s — a classic of UK roots**
- **Played Twinkle Brothers selections at every dance; Norman a regular**
- First contact with Polish radio journalist Kliszcz happened at Jah Shaka's shop (confirmed in 2023 Maken interview)
- NME quote (Vivien Goldman, 1981): "When the other sounds had done with their boasting and toasting, there would come a discreet hiss from the corner, and Shaka would mutter a title, and the walls seemed to be tumbling down around your ears."

### Reggaeville bio
https://reggaeville.com
- Anglican Diocese baptism; choir from age 6 and 8
- Pop and Mento competitions: parish champions; "cheated" two years running at regional level; won third attempt
- The Cardinals: mixed-class Falmouth band; Norman & Ralston only "ghetto boys"
- First recording: Norman solo "Somebody Please Help Me" — Leslie Kong, Beverly's
- First group single: "Matthew and Mark" — Duke Reid, Treasure Isle
- 1970: Jack Pot label → Dynamic Sounds, Federal Records, Sun Shot, Upsetter labels
- 1970: formed own Twinkle label
- Festivals: Essential Festival Brighton, Bob Marley Days, Reggae on the River, Sunsplash, Helsinki Reggae Festival, Holland's Reggae Eruption, Germany, Poland, Spain, Canada

### Discogs profile

### AllMusic bio (John Dougan / Rovi)

- *Rasta Pon Top* (1975): "rasta-infused, roots-heavy demi-masterpiece with soul and gospel vocal stylings"
- Mid-90s Poland collaboration → *Twinkle Inna Poland Style*
